Phasmophobia

Image credit: MelodicBeam's Video

Phasmophobia is a ghost investigation game where the player is tasked with entering a map, collecting evidence, and using that evidence to determine what type of ghost is present.

Image credit: Game Skinny

But the thing that makes this horror game really shine, in my opinion, is that you can play the game entirely without collecting evidence and still be 100% certain about the ghost type.

In 3 of the 5 difficulties in the game, the ghost will have a special interaction with three "evidence items" in the game. The player can then fill these in using their journal, which will tell them the exact ghost type. But, for players with an extensive knowledge of the game, it is possible to determine the ghost type with less than three evidence, or even with no evidence.

Each ghost type has it's own differences in behavior during different aspects of the game. For example, some ghosts can hunt early under certain conditions, meaning they can hunt (try to kill the player) when the player has a sanity higher than 50. (Sanity starts at 100 and slowly decreases throughout the game based on conditions such as interacting with the ghost and standing in darkness.) For example, a demon can hunt below 70 sanity, well above the 50 where most ghosts can hunt. Demons also have a rare special ability allowing them to initiate a hunt at any sanity level. Thus, if a player notices a ghost hunt almost immediately, it may indicate that the ghost is a demon. Another type of ghost, a reventant, will hunt at half speed, until it gets line of sight with the player, at which point it will hunt at double speed. If a player notices this speed difference, they could identify the ghost as a revenant without collecting evidence. This game has a total of 24 ghost types, and they all have complex and interesting interactions with the various systems in the game, leading some to even consider it a puzzle game.
